About Bali

Bali is one of the provinces in Indonesia. Bali is located between Java Island and Lombok Island. The provincial capital is Denpasar, which is located in the southern part of the island. Bali Island is part of the 253 km long and 112 km wide Lesser Sunda Islands, about 3.2 km from Java Island. It is located at the coordinates 08°03'40" - 08°50'48" N and 114°25'53" - 115°42'40" WEST. The total area of Bali Province is 5,636.66 km2 or 0.29 percent of the Indonesian archipelago. Geographically, Bali Province is located at 8°3'40" - 8°50'48" South latitude and 114°25'53" - 115°42'40" East Longitude. The relief and topography of Bali Island in the center of the mountain range extends from west to east. Bali Province is located between Java Island and Lombok Island..

Bali Province consists of several islands, namely Bali Island as the largest island, Nusa Penida Island, Nusa Ceningan Island, Nusa Lembongan Island, Serangan Island (located around the foot of Bali Island), and Menjangan Island located in the western part of Bali Island.

History Of Bali

The culture and language of the Balinese are closely related to those of the Indonesian archipelago, Malaysia, the Philippines and Osenia. In historical records stone tools dating back to ancient Bali were found near the village of Cekik - Jembrana in the West of the island. The ancient culture of old Bali itself was heavily influenced by Indian and Chinese culture, especially Hinduism. The name Bali Dwipa was also given to the island, as found in a number of inscriptions. One of them is the Blanjong inscription written by Sri Kesari Warmadewa in 914 AD which mentions "walidwipa" so the history of the name Bali can be found in an inscription. The history of ancient Bali also records that the island was also controlled by Dutch colonizers, namely in 1597, Cornelis De Houtman from the Dutch colonial colonizers arrived in Bali with the formation of the Dutch East India company in 1602..

Upacara Ngaben

Ngaben ceremony is a funeral pyre ceremony in Bali which is believed by the Balinese Hindu community as a ritual to perfect the corpse back to the Creator. Ngaben ceremony is divided into three types, namely: Ngaben sawa Wedana, Ngaben Asti Wedana, and Private. The Ngaben Sawa Wedana ceremony is performed after the body is preserved before the burning ritual takes place. Meanwhile, Ngaben Asti Wedana is performed after the body is buried first. Finally, the Swasta ceremony is performed for Balinese who died outside the region or whose bodies were not found.

Hari Raya Saraswati

Hari Raya Saraswati is a holiday celebrating knowledge. On this holiday, Balinese Hindus usually perform special ceremonies to worship or glorify Dewi Saraswati who is believed to bring knowledge on earth to make everyone in the world smart and educated. Everything related to science such as books and books are prayed for in the Saraswati ceremony. Not only that, dance performances and story readings are usually performed all night long.

Hari Raya Galungan

Galungan comes from the Old Javanese language and means 'Win'. In accordance with the origin of its name, this traditional ceremony in Bali aims to celebrate victory against evil. In addition, the Galungan ceremony is also held to commemorate the creation of the universe and its contents. The series of Galungan holidays has been going on for about 25 days before Galungan Day. Every 210 days of the Balinese calendar, Hindus in Bali celebrate Galungan Day.

Mekare-Kare

Mekare-Kare is performed annually to honor Indra, the Hindu god of war. By performing this ceremony, all men in the village will be considered strong and capable of waging war. The ceremony will usually be held in early June each year.

Upacara Mekotek

This ceremony is held right after Kuningan Day, or after Galungan Day. This tradition begins with prayers at Pura Dalem Munggu, which is then followed by a parade to the water source by carrying a mountain-like bamboo assembly.

Upacara Tumpek Landep

This ceremony will be led by traditional leaders, and carried out in a temple that is considered sacred and has an appropriate location. Usually this ceremony is held once a year, coinciding with Tumpek Landep Day according to the Balinese calendar.

Tari Kecak

Kecak dance is a very famous Balinese dance. This dance is played by tens or even hundreds of male dancers who form a circle formation. The main feature of kecak dance is the simultaneous shouting of the word "cak cak cak" by the dancers with two hands up.

Tari Pendet

Pendet dance is a cult dance that is often performed in temples by Hindus as part of a worship procession to welcome the arrival of the Gods from the sky. This type of Balinese dance is performed by a certain number of female dancers wearing Balinese traditional clothes. The dancers are also equipped with flower decorations and carry offerings.

Tari Barong

This Balinese dance is performed by dancers wearing barong costumes with scary faces decorated with Balinese ornaments. The word barong in this dance is thought to come from the word "bahruang" or meaning bear. Although it comes from the bear deed, there are many other mask models, such as barong asu, baron blablasan, barong elephant, barong tiger and so on. By some people, barong masks sometimes also become home decorations.

Tari Condong

According to the beliefs passed down from generation to generation, Condong dance is a Balinese dance that originated from the dream of a prince from Sukawati when he was sick in the 19th century. At that time, he dreamed of meeting two beautiful girls who were dancing with graceful movements.

Tari Wiranata

Wiranata dance is a Balinese dance creation created by I Nyoman Ridet in 1960. This dance depicts a heroic figure who conveys a message and impression of dashing, authority and influence. This dance is generally danced by female dancers, although it can also be danced by male dancers. The dancers can perform solo or in groups.

Tari Janger

Janger dance is a Balinese dance that is included in the youth association dance group which is believed to have existed since 1930. This dance is performed by pairs of male and female dancers totaling 10 to 16 people. The dancers also sing the janger song in unison.

1.Berpakaian dan Berperilaku Sopan

The people of Bali tend to be more conservative by sticking to traditional values and ancestral culture. As a tourist, you are a guest who must respect and abide by some of these things. Try to wear modest clothing that covers the shoulders, especially when visiting temples and religious rituals. Maintain good manners while on Bali Island by not making rude remarks or making out in public.

2.Jangan Pernah Merusak Sesajen

When on the island of Bali, you will often see many offerings placed in front of buildings or under trees. Usually the offerings consist of ubo rampe flowers and incense wrapped in banana leaves. For the Balinese, these offerings are a form of respect for the universe. It is illegal for you to damage these offerings if you do not want to experience trance and be disturbed by spirits.

3.Jangan Mengganggu Ritual Ibadah

The people of Bali Island quite regularly hold worship rituals in some of Bali's main temples. If you visit a temple that is being used for worship rituals, make sure your tourist activities do not interfere with the solemnity of the ongoing worship ritual procession.

4.Jangan Memasuki Pura saat Haid

A temple is a sacred place used to perform various religious rituals. Temples may only be entered by people in a state of purity. Women who are menstruating according to the Balinese people are considered to be unchaste so they are prohibited from entering the temple. In addition, tourists who are bleeding due to wounds on their bodies are also not prohibited from entering the temple.

5.Mengenakan Kain saat Memasuki Pura

There is one rule that must be obeyed by tourists who want to enter the temple, namely wearing a shawl and cloth. Usually visitors who have worn pants or skirts that cover the knees will only be required to use a shawl wrapped around the hips. But for tourists who wear pants or short skirts above the knee, before wearing a shawl will be required to use a cloth to cover the thighs first.

6.Hormati Hari Raya Nyepi

Nyepi Day is one of the religious rituals in the Hindu religion by avoiding activities in the crowd. During Nyepi Day, Balinese people will stay indoors, even they will not turn on the lights at night. If your trip to Bali coincides with the Nyepi Day celebration, be sure to respect the ritual by not making noise and turning on electrical appliances in public places.
